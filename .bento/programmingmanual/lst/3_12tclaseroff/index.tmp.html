Laser method Depending on the parameters entered, the following machine commands are executed with the TC_LASER_OFF cycle call-up: ■ Switch off the laser beam. ■ Switch off the cutting gas. ■ Position the Z axis. The TC_LASER_OFF cycle is called up with a cycle parameter in the NC program: TC_LASER_OFF(Laser method) The following TC_LASER_OFF cycles are available:

|Laser method | a|

Functions |1 | a| Beam OFF, Z axis at overshoot height (standard overshoot height = 40 mm). |2 | a| Beam OFF, high voltage OFF, Z axis at home position. |3 | a| Corresponds to method 1. |4 | a| Beam OFF, Z axis remains at processing clearance (at piercing height for the following piercings / at cutting height for the following cuts). |6 | a| In the case of RotoLas: Laser beam OFF, Z axis to overtravel height (in relation to the outside diameter). |10 | a| Beam OFF, preparation for hard Microweld. |11 | a| Beam OFF, preparation for soft Microweld. = Tab. 5-8 The laser methods are used as follows:

■ If Laser method 1 is programmed, the overtravel height which the Z axis is to approach must previously be programmed with TC_POS_LEVEL (TruLaser series 7000: the cutting heads taking part in the cutting process). This method is used to switch off the laser beam after a processed part and to position the next part.

■ Laser method 2 is used to switch off the laser beam at the end of part programming and to move the Z axis to the home position.

■ Laser method 3 corresponds to Laser method 1.

■ Laser method 4 is used for "common cuts" processing and for SprintLine. Under no circumstances may a hole or formed section be moved over during positioning.
